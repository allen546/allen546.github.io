The Blue Whale

What is the biggest animal in the world? If your answer is the Blue Whale, you're right. One day, I was surfing the web, and I accidentally found an article about blue whales on nationalgeographic.com. It was an interesting article, so I started searching the web for "Blue Whale". I found a lot of interesting stuff there, like the ICUN red list level of the blue whale is "critically endangered". Today, I am going tell you about Blue Whales.

The blue whale is a subspecies of whales and they, just like other whales, are threatened by environmental changes such as habitat loss and toxics. They are also harmed by ship strikes, becoming entangled in fiching gear and the rising sea levels. Whales are at the top of the food chain and plays an important role in the overall health of the marine environment. But the main reason is because of human activity. And that's why we should protect the Blue Whale.

The words "blue whale" is the spieces's common name. Its' scientific name is "Balaenoptera musculus". There are four subspecies of the Blue Whale. They are B. musculus musculus, B. musculus intermedia, B. musculus indica, and B. musculus brevicauda.

All Whales are Mammals. Blue whales are the largest animals ever known to have lived on Earth. These magnificent marine mammals rule the oceans a up to 100 feet long and upwards of 200 tons. Their tongues alone can weigh as much as an elephant. Their hearts? as much as an automobile.

Their Diet includes krill, which is a tiny shrimplike animal. During a year, a single adult blue whale consumes around 4 tons of krill each day. First, The 200-ton thing eat by first swallowing a giant mouthful of water. Then, it will process the water by running the water through the gills of the blue whale, which will keep the krill in, but push the rest of the water into the ocean. Then, they will force the krill down into their tummies.
